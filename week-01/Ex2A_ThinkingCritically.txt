Difference between a “search term” and a “category”
When you search something in Google Trends as a search term, it only shows results for the exact word or phrase people type in Google. When you search it as a category or topic, it includes many related searches, even if people use different words.
For example, “Milkshake (search term)” only shows searches that say “milkshake.” But “Milkshake (beverage)” includes other searches like chocolate milkshake, strawberry milkshake, or milkshake recipes.
This made me realize that when working with Google Trends data, it is important to choose the right option. If you only use a search term, you might miss other related searches. Using a category can give a bigger picture of what people are actually searching for.
